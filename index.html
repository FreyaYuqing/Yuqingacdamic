Data-driven design for enhancing cultural tourism experiences and innovating IP in ethnic regions

This study intend to justified that innovative IP design can enhance visitor satisfaction in the cultural tourism market. A unique aspect of the research is its data-driven approach to creating tourism products that align with visitors' emotional responses, accommodating the cultural characteristics of heritage and visitor expectations.

The methodology applies Python for text mining and sentiment analysis on online travel reviews, using Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) to identify key emotional responses. Findings reveal that visitor emotions are complex and influenced by factors such as IP imagery design, authenticity of cultural representation, and service experience, with strong appreciation expressed for immersive and authentic experiences.

By leveraging technology and emotional insights, tourism developers can offer more engaging and culturally enriched experiences.

Keywords:

Ethnic Cultural Tourism, Sentiment Analysis, Intellectual Property, Data-driven Design

Leveraging AR/VR Technologies in Advancing Urban Digital Transformation

The research mainly explores the application of Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) technologies in urban planning and their impact on public participation willingness and feedback effectiveness. By integrating AR/VR technologies into specific decision-making stages of urban planning, this study aims to enhance public understanding and participation in complex urban planning concepts.

The research design revolves around several key scientific questions:

  • First, the research team focuses on how AR/VR technologies can help the public better understand urban planning proposals, especially how these technologies improve spatial perception through 3D visualization.
  • Secondly, the research examines whether public willingness to participate in urban planning activities increases after experiencing AR/VR technology, and whether this technology makes them more actively engaged in urban planning discussions and decisions.
  • In addition, the study evaluates the impact of technological intervention on decision quality, exploring whether the use of AR/VR technologies improves the quality of public feedback and contributes to the formulation of high-quality urban planning decisions.

Master's Thesis

Exploring the relationship between natural resources and mental health: a case study of Sheffield's informal green space

Research Goal

Green space, as an environmental resource, can contribute to mental health has been well justified. But at the same time the adverse effects of green space gentrification are threatening environmental equality. In order to alleviate the unequal distribution of green space resources, informal green spaces are being looked at because of their high accessibility and low maintenance costs. The aim of this research is to explore whether informal green spaces, like formal green spaces, also contribute to mental health.

Research Questions

Could informal green space be a complement to formal green space to provide support for mental health? To what extent can it be seen as a potential health resource?

Methodology

Quantitative and qualitative research approach were adopted to clarify the relationship between informal green spaces and mental health. The demographic and behaviour date was got from "Improving Well-being through Urban Nature" project conducted by Derby University. The study's main challenge lies in distinguishing formal and informal green spaces on geographic information maps. To achieve this, green space data from the Digimap Ordnance Survey (OS MasterMap), which includes details on facilities and vegetation types within green areas, was utilized. The resulting green space boundaries were then overlaid with real-time geofencing data from participants, providing insights into the behaviors of users in both formal and informal green spaces.

Results

The results found no significant correlation between the type of green space used (formal or informal) and the mental health status of the participants. This suggested that the evidence for informal green spaces as a health resource is not yet sufficient. However, the statistical analysis did reveal correlations between age, childhood exposure to nature, access to private gardens, and positive emotional outcomes. This highlights a promising direction for research and investment in informal green spaces, specifically examining how different age groups engage with these areas.

Limitation

This result may also be due to limitations in accurately distinguishing between formal and informal green spaces. Future research could address this by incorporating field observations to refine area classifications further.
